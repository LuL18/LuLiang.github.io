introduction

Date: Sept. 2020
Time: 4 Months
Role: Product Design

Artzle as an educational game is designed based on the special need of society for art creation and art education during the pandemic outbreak and quarantine. The design concept uses research-creation methodologies. It focuses on special social situations, and its aim is to maximize the positive influence and effects of art and technology through the design and use of this game.

I designed this project on my own, including the research, analysis, ideation, illustration creation and visual design.

Research

I began the project by committing interviews. My interviewees include two groups of people. The interviewees in the first group are my friends, aged between 20 and 30, most of whom are studying abroad. The second group is about children aged 8-14 years and their parents. I did more interviews with the first group.

Pain Points

I summarized the main problems my interviewees were facing. These problems include but are not limited to:

1. Have to cancle all the travel plans.

2. Lack of family activities because public activities are not available, such as exhibitions and workshops in museums.

3. Unable to be reunited with families and friends.

4. nable to socialize normally, including meeting new people and hanging out with friends.

Problem &
Design Requirements

I summarized a problem statement by using the research and synthesis as the guideline:

   The requirement of normal social interaction during the      quarantine.


Based on the problem, I developed a list of design requirements for solving the problem. This mobile game is supposed to:

1. Help users to express themselves and release pressure through artistic creation.
2. Encourage users to keep communicating with families and friends, and meet new people.
3. Using technology to help users to explore the world.
4. Let users play and learn at the same time.

Reflection

I had only four months for this project from the idea to launch, and I had to finish it all by myself, so I needed to be as efficient as possible. Although sometimes I paid too much attention to the little details, I learned that not everything could get done, and sometimes making compromises is necessary for a project to move forward.

The program was designed primarily for art education purposes. If I were to continue this project, I would expand more possibilities in other areas to improve its functionality. In addition, due to time and resource constraints, I could not test the final project enough times. It would be very helpful to do more usability testing with real users and receive more feedback.
